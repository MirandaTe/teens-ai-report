A deep-dive visual investigation into Pew Research Center's 2026 study of 1,458 U.S. teens and their parents.

Use Cases

The Spectrum of Adoption

Teens are treating AI as a "Universal Assistant." While 57% use it for Information Seeking, the long-tail includes everything from Content Creation (38%) to Emotional Support (12%).

The "Rose" visualization shows the radial density of these different life-integrations.

The Income Reliance

Teens in households earning <$30k are 3x more likely to do "all or most" work with AI than those earning $75k+.

1 in 10

Heavy Users

10% of all teens now complete "all or most" of their school assignments using AI chatbots.

03

The Value Gap

Humanity's Edge

Teens see a clear hierarchy. AI wins at Teaching Skills (34% Better), but loses heavily in Hiring (47% Worse) and Medical Diagnosis (38% Worse).

The Diverging Chart tracks where teens trust the algorithm—and where they don't.

04

Demographics

The Cultural Divide

Black and Hispanic teens lead the way in using AI for Searching Information (61%) and News (29%), significantly outpacing their White peers.

Usage is not a monolith; it is shaped by race, ethnicity, and gender identity.

05

Boundaries

The Empathy Wall

Parents are "OK" with AI as a tool, but "NOT OK" with AI as a companion. 58% reject AI for Emotional Support, and 45% reject it for Casual Conversation.
